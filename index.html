How to read this

“Feels like” is a nighttime Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT): it combines air temperature and humidity, because humid heat prevents the body from cooling by sweating. With no sun at night, WBGT ≈ 0.7 × wet-bulb temperature + 0.3 × air temperature. It is usually a few degrees below the air temperature — so a feels-like of 25° is seriously oppressive.

An overnight window runs 21:00 to 09:00 the next morning, in each city's local time. Each night is labeled by its evening date.

≥ 20° feels-like all night: hot night, disturbed sleep likely. ≥ 25° feels-like all night: dangerous heat, little overnight relief — a serious health risk for older people and those with medical conditions.

Uncertainty: the “hours ≥ threshold” columns show the median across the 40-member ICON weather ensemble, with the 10th–90th percentile range in brackets — e.g. “4 h (2–6)” means most ensemble members expect 2 to 6 hours at or above the threshold.

Data: Open-Meteo (deterministic forecast + ICON ensemble), updated every 3 hours. Static site, no tracking. Source on GitHub.

Forecasts are indicative, not an official heat warning. Follow national meteorological services for alerts.
